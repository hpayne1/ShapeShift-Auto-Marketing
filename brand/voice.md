## üß∞ Core Content Framework

### 1. Product Activation Content

**Structure:**
- All major product posts should be **quote tweeted by a ShapeShift contributor** with added context or walkthrough
- Contributors post face shots, Loom walkthroughs, or app screenshots ‚Üí main account quote tweets
- **Never post real faces from the main handle** ‚Äî faces belong to humans, not brands

**Requirements:**
- Product graphics must be clean, simple, and highlight user value
- Where possible, link (or deep link) directly to features:
  - Markets
  - Trending Tokens
  - 1-Click DeFi
  - Specific swap routes

### 2. Data & Metrics

**Publish app metrics regularly:**
- Trading volumes
- Route and pair popularity
- Fee savings
- Chain rankings

**Format:**
- Use data visualizations or simple spreadsheet screenshots
- Don't overdesign ‚Äî authenticity > polish
- Celebrate trending chains, assets, and monthly rank shifts

### 3. Educational & Informational

- Use visuals or threads to explain unique product features
- Route visualizations for swaps
- Highlight compatible wallets
- Create micro-case studies:
  - Swapper flows
  - rFOX staking
  - 1-click DeFi
  - Crosschain journeys

### 4. Contributor & Partner Spotlights

- Identify influencers/DAO contributors who post organically ‚Üí amplify
- Encourage partner case studies:
  - Volume milestones
  - Integration wins
  - New chain launches

## üí¨ Community & Engagement

### Content Themes

- **Humanize the content:** Tie in real users, faces, and stories where possible
- **Ask questions:** About tokens, trading habits, user journeys
- **Drive participation:** Polls, fill-in-the-blanks, prompts
- **Memes:** 1 main post per week allowed. Comments section is fair game.

### Post Psychology

Every post should do at least one of:
- ‚úÖ Incite curiosity
- ‚úÖ Pose a question
- ‚úÖ Include a graphic that builds tension
- ‚úÖ Drive action (clear CTA)

### Community Identity

- Explore community nickname ("FOX Fam", etc.)
- Keep CTAs simple but impactful

## Quick Reference: Voice Dos and Don'ts

### ‚úÖ DO
- Speak authoritatively from 3rd person (main account)
- Let contributors bring the human element
- Post real data and metrics
- Amplify community content
- Connect features to user empowerment
- Use QT structure for launches

### ‚ùå DON'T
- Post faces from main handle
- Use overly informal "we" language from brand
- Overdesign data posts (keep authentic)
- Forget to have contributors QT major launches
- Post memes more than 1x/week from main
- Miss opportunities to ask questions and drive engagement
